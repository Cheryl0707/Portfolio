Music Recitals in Holland Park

We were inspired by Holland park since this location is very void and spacious, there is a lot of sunlight that is able to enter the pavilion. As our main theme of the pavilion design is nature, we hope that more natural light could enter the interior space and so we have chosen a location that could get in touch with the most sunlight during day time. That could help enrich users' experience on the aspect of nature when they enter our pavilion. Furthermore, since there is a large blank meadow facing the front entrance, users can actually see sceneries, buildings, and trees from the far side. With the use of grass, we have created an open but also private space for people to enjoy their time within the pavilion. Overall, with the help of natural lights and sceneries, users can enjoy the time and space inside our pavilion by temporary putting down the oppression that daily life brought them.

​Site: Holland Park

Why we select this particular site We select this particular location because of two factors, the size of the location and its flow of people. We want to make sure that everyone who enters the park could at least see and walk past our pavilion. We think that the walkway right next to the location we have chosen is a main and must-pass walkway of the park; We also want to ensure the size of the location is large enough to fit the Pavilion and its surroundings pergola, in the meanwhile able to maximize the pavilion.

Production

We make many sketches and variations to integrate gothic styles in our speaker.

We extract the gothic decorative elements and the colorful glass window. Ideally, we want the wood part to be Laser cut into some traditional patterns on the model's back wood part. Users can choose to touch a button on the front to turn on the light. Light can also pass through the gaps to create a sense of atmosphere.

In order to get beautiful decorations on the wood panels, we use laser cutting technology to outline the patterns. The patterns and panels shapes are designed in a gothic style, which is highly ornamental and geometrical.
