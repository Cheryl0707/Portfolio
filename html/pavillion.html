Music Recitals

The musical recital is a relax and chill open space in Holland Park. We sought to integrate nature into our design ethos. Our emphasis on nature led us to strategically position the pavilion to maximize natural light penetration, creating an interior space that connects intimately with the sun's radiance throughout the day. This deliberate choice enhances the visitors' immersion in the natural surroundings upon entering the pavilion.

Project Type

3D Design

Roles

Pergola Designer / Twinmotion Operator / Video Editor

Tools

Intro

This visionary project stands as a testament to the seamless integration of nature, art, and architecture, encapsulating the essence of both creativity and functionality. Its design is a harmonious blend of organic forms and contemporary aesthetics, mirroring the intricate patterns found in nature's own creations.

​Site: Holland Park

Why we select this particular site because of two main factors, the size of the location and its flow of people. We want to make sure that everyone who enters the park could at least see and walk past our pavilion. We think that the walkway right next to the location we have chosen is a main and must-pass walkway of the park; We also want to ensure the size of the location is large enough to fit the Pavilion and its surroundings pergola, in the meanwhile able to maximize the pavilion.

3D Modeling

Rhino Model for Pavilion

In the design of the Pavilion's roof, we skillfully blend the concept of the trunk's annual rings. Drawing inspiration from the intricate growth patterns found within the cross-section of a tree trunk, our pavilion's roof emulates the very essence of nature's time-honored cycles. Our aim is to symbolize the profound connection between music and trees—both leaving indelible imprints on history. Similar to the annual rings that chronicle a tree's growth, music too leaves its mark throughout the annals of time.
